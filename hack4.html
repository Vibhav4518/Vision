Title:know Your Constitution - Citizen Edition

Instructions: Choose the best answer. Each question has one correct option. No stress-Just learning!

1. What is the Constitution of India?

A) A list of rules for cricket.

B) A book of laws for the citizen and goverment.

C) A religion text.

D) A cookbook.

ANSWER:B

2. Which of the following is a Fundamental Right?

A) Right to Gossip.

B) Right to Property

C) Right to Equality.

D) Right to Drive

ANSWER:C

3.What does "Right to Equality" mean?

A) Everyone must wear the same clothes.

B) Everyone is treated equally before the law.

C) Everyone must have the same job.

D) Everyone eats the same food.

ANSWER:B

5. Can a Citizen express their opinion freely in India?

A) No, only in private.

B) Yes, it's a Fundamental Right.

C) Only during elections.

D) Only on social media.

ANSWER:B

6. What is a citizen's duty under the Constitution.

A) To sleep early.

B) To respect the national flag and anthem.

C) To never question anything.

D) To ignore laws.

ANSWER:B

7. What does the Preamble of the Constitution do?

A) Lists names of leaders.

B) Tells a story.

C) Describe the aims and spirit of the Constitution.

D) Acts as a table of contents.

ANSWER:C

8. Which value is not mentioned in the Preamble?

A) Justice.

B) Liberty.

C) Fashion.

D) Equality.

ANSWER:C

9. Can laws be made against the Constitution?

A) Yes,if needed.

B) No, Constitution is the highest law.

C) Maybe during holidays.

D) Only if politicians agree.

ANSWER:B

10. Who makes laws in India.

A) Citizen directly.

B) The Parliament.

C) Judges.

D) Bolloywood actors.

ANSWER:B

11.How many Fundamental Rights are given in the Constitution?

A) 3

B) 6

C) 10

D) 12

ANSWER:B

12. What does "Secular" mean in the Indian Constitution?

A) Belief in only one religion.

B) No belief in any relogion.

C) Equal respect to all religions.

D) Everyone must follow the same religion.

ANSWER:C

13. Who is known as the 'Father of the Indian Constitution.'

A) Mahatma Gandhi

B) Subhash Chandra Bose

C) Dr. B.R Ambedkar

D) Jawaharlal Nehru

ANSWER:C

14. Which of these is not a Fundamental Duty?

A) Protect the environment.

B) Respect national symbols.

C) Pay income tax.

D) Promote harmony.

ANSWER:C(Paying tax is legal duty,not listed as a Fundamental Duty)

14. What is the minimum age to vote in India?

A) 21years

B) 16years

C) 18years

D) 25years

15.The Constitution came into effect on:

A) 15th August 1947

B) 26th January 1950

C) 2nd Octobar 1948

D) 26th Novembar 1949

16. What is the rule of law?

A) Only rich people follow laws.

B) Everyone is equal before the law.

C) Police can make laws.

D) Laws change for each person.

17. Which part of the Constitution contains Fundamental Rights?

A) Preamble

B) PART III

C) Part IV

D) Schedule 7

18. What is the meaning of "Republic"?

A) King rules forever.

B) Citizen elect their leaders.

C) Only army rules.

D) Religious leaders govern.

19. Can Fundamental Rights be taken away during emergency?

A) No

B) Yes, some rights can be restricted.

C) Only with public vote.

20. Who signs the laws passed by Parliament to make them official?

A) Prime Minister.

B) Chief Justice.

A) Only goverment official.

B) Only cities.
